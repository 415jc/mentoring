# Mentor README

## About

I am a Principal Software Architect and I am a generalist.

My experience spans multiple areas of software engineering, including technical leadership, system design, architecture, cross-functional collaboration, and navigating ambiguity in engineering organizations.

## Scope of Mentorship

Topics may include:

- Software engineering career growth
- Technical leadership
- System design and architecture
- Engineering strategy
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Organizational navigation
- Staff+ and principal-level expectations
- Navigating ambiguity and tradeoffs
- Communication and influence

As a generalist, I may be most useful in helping connect concepts across domains rather than focusing narrowly on a single technology area.

## Mentorship Sessions

Mentees may come with:

- Specific questions
- Topics they want to discuss
- Situations they want perspective on
- Or no agenda at all

Sessions do not require preparation to be useful.

## Expectations of the Mentee

Mentees are expected to:

- Be respectful of time commitments
- Bring relevant context when asking for advice
- Be open to discussion and feedback
- Treat mentorship as collaborative rather than transactional
- Own the calendar item, including:
  - Creating the invite
  - Adding the meeting link
  - Rescheduling if necessary
  - Cancelling if plans change

Mentorship discussions are intended to help with decision-making, tradeoff analysis, and perspective, not to make decisions on behalf of the mentee.

## Communication Style

My approach is discussion-oriented and based on tradeoffs, context, and decision-making frameworks rather than prescriptive answers.

I may challenge assumptions, ask clarifying questions, or offer alternative perspectives when useful.

I will also say when I do not know something or when a topic falls outside my experience.

## Psychological Safety & Feedback Norms

Questions are welcome, including basic or exploratory questions.

Disagreement is acceptable and expected in technical discussions. The goal is constructive discussion rather than consensus for its own sake.

Feedback may be direct, but it is intended to be respectful, actionable, and grounded in context.

## Confidentiality & Boundaries

Conversations are expected to remain professional.

Mentees should not disclose anything illegal or unethical during mentorship sessions. If information involving legal, ethical, compliance, harassment, safety, or policy violations is shared, I may have a responsibility to report it through the appropriate channels.
